The Ecosystem

Three roles.
One open network.

Miners produce intelligence. Validators score it. Stakers back it. Together they form a self-sustaining, permissionless AI economy powered by TAO.

Role 01

Mine intelligence.
Earn TAO.

Miners are the backbone of the NeuralChain network. They run AI models and provide real, useful computational outputs in response to tasks sent through the network.

The better your outputs — the more accurate, the faster, the more reliable — the higher your TAO rewards. Competition is global, open, and purely merit-based.

How to become a miner

01
💻
Set Up Hardware

You need a GPU with at least 8GB VRAM for most subnets. More powerful hardware enables more subnets and higher throughput.

02
🔧
Install the SDK

Install the NeuralChain SDK via pip, create a wallet, and register on your chosen subnet by paying a small registration fee in TAO.

03
🚀
Start Mining

Deploy your miner script, connect to the subnet, and begin responding to tasks. The network will score your outputs and reward you accordingly.

Role 02

Score the work.
Keep it honest.

Validators are the quality assurance layer of the NeuralChain network. They continuously evaluate miner outputs and submit scores that determine who gets rewarded.

Accurate, honest validators earn more. The Yuma Consensus mechanism is designed to make collusion unprofitable — precision is always the best strategy.

How Yuma Consensus works

01
📋
Receive Outputs

Validators receive outputs from all active miners on a subnet. Each validator independently evaluates the quality of each output against the subnet's standards.

02
🔢
Submit Scores

Each validator submits a weight vector ranking miners by output quality. These rankings are combined using the Shapley value framework to produce consensus scores.

03
💰
Rewards Distributed

TAO emissions flow to miners and validators based on consensus scores. Validators who score accurately earn more — creating a permanent incentive for honest evaluation.

Role 03

Stake TAO.
Back the future.

Stakers are the investors of the NeuralChain ecosystem. By staking TAO into subnets, they signal which AI markets deserve greater resource allocation.

Dynamic TAO uses an automated market maker for each subnet — staking TAO into a subnet increases its value and your proportional reward share. You become a Decentralized Cloud Provider.

How staking works

01
🔍
Choose a Subnet

Research the active subnets, their performance metrics, and their potential. Pick the subnets whose mission and output quality you believe in.

02
💎
Stake TAO

Deposit TAO into your chosen subnet's reserve via the Dynamic TAO AMM. Your stake increases the subnet's market value and your proportional share of its emissions.

03
📊
Earn Rewards

As the subnet performs well and grows, emissions flow proportionally to stakers. You can unstake anytime and vote on governance proposals with your staked weight.

Common Questions

Everything you need
to get started.

How much TAO can I earn as a miner? +

Earnings depend heavily on your hardware, the subnet you choose, and the quality of your outputs relative to other miners. Top miners on competitive subnets can earn significant TAO daily, but there's no guaranteed amount — it's a merit-based competition.

Do I need to run a validator to participate? +

No. You can participate as a miner with just a GPU, or as a staker with any amount of TAO. Validation requires a minimum TAO stake and more technical knowledge, but it's not required to earn rewards.

What hardware do I need to mine? +

Requirements vary by subnet. Most language model subnets require a GPU with at least 8GB VRAM (e.g., RTX 3090, 4090, or A100). Some subnets are more CPU-friendly. Check individual subnet documentation for specific requirements.

Is staking safe? What are the risks? +

Staking carries market risk — the value of TAO can go up or down. There's no slashing risk for regular stakers (slashing only applies to validators who collude). You can unstake at any time, though there may be a cooldown period.

Can I participate from anywhere in the world? +

Yes. NeuralChain is a permissionless, global network. Anyone with internet access and the required hardware (or TAO to stake) can participate — there are no geographic restrictions.
